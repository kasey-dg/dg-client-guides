European Trip Packing List

Pack smart for cobblestones, cafés, and everything in between!  ✓ = Check it off as you go.

Travel Documents & Money
  • Passport (valid 6+ months past return date)
  • Printed flight & hotel confirmations
  • Travel insurance policy & emergency contact
  • ETIAS authorization (if applicable)
  • Credit cards with no foreign transaction fees
  • Small amount of local currency (cash)
  • Driver’s license / International Driving Permit (if renting a car)
  • Prescription copies / medical info
  • Photocopy of passport (kept separate from original)
Pro Tip Email yourself digital copies of your passport, ID, and confirmations — if anything goes missing, you’ll have backup in seconds.
Anti-Theft Essentials
  • Crossbody purse / bag with anti-theft features (slash-proof strap, locking zippers)
  • RFID-blocking wallet or sleeve
  • Money belt or hidden travel pouch for backup cash/cards
  • Front-facing day bag (avoid back pockets & open totes in crowds)
  • Small padlock for hotel lockers or luggage
  • Decoy wallet with a little cash (optional, but smart in high-pickpocket areas)
Pro Tip Wear your crossbody bag across your front, not your back, in crowded squares, train stations, and markets — that’s where pickpockets work the hardest.
Tech & Electronics
  • Phone + charger
  • Camera + memory cards
  • Headphones / earbuds
  • E-reader or tablet
  • European plug adapter (Type C/E/F or Type G for UK)
  • Portable power bank
  • Offline maps downloaded in advance
Misc Essentials
  • Reusable water bottle (refill — many cities have public fountains)
  • Compact umbrella
  • Packable day bag or tote
  • Stain remover pen
  • Reusable shopping bag (many shops charge for bags or don’t provide them)
  • Outlet adapter splitter (hotel outlets are often limited)
  • Earplugs / eye mask (cobblestone streets + church bells = early mornings)
  • Notebook & pen
  • Snacks for train rides or long travel days
Clothing & Shoes
  • Comfortable walking shoes with great grip (cobblestones are no joke!)
  • 1 pair broken-in sneakers + 1 dressier pair for evenings
  • Layers: t-shirts, light sweaters, breathable button-downs
  • 1–2 nicer outfits for fine dining or theater nights
  • Versatile dress or two (day-to-night friendly)
  • Lightweight scarf (chic + handy for cooler churches/cathedrals)
  • Packable rain jacket
  • Light cardigan or jacket for cool evenings
  • Jeans or trousers (more common than shorts in many cities)
  • Pajamas / loungewear
  • Undergarments & socks (extra pair for wet weather days)
Pro Tip Skip the brand-new shoes! Break them in for at least 2 weeks before you go — cobblestones and miles of walking will find every weak spot.
Toiletries & Health
  • Sunscreen
  • Travel-size shampoo, conditioner, body wash
  • Deodorant & personal care items
  • Toothbrush, toothpaste, floss
  • Makeup & brushes
  • Hair tools (check voltage — many run on 220V, look for dual-voltage)
  • Prescription medications (extra supply, in original bottles)
  • Pain relievers, antacids, allergy meds
  • Motion sickness remedies for trains/ferries
  • Blister pads & moleskin (see clothing tip above!)
  • Travel-size hand sanitizer & tissues (not all restrooms are stocked)
Genius Travel Hacks & Europe Tips
  • Packing cubes (you’ll be living out of your suitcase across multiple cities!)
  • A printed list of your hotel addresses in the local language (for taxis)
  • Coins or small bills set aside for pay-to-use restrooms
  • Portable luggage scale (avoid surprise fees on budget flights between cities)
  • Collapsible tote for souvenirs on the way home
  • Translation app downloaded for offline use
  • Laundry sheets or travel detergent for longer trips
  • A lightweight daypack that folds flat in your suitcase
  • Hand fan (many European hotels & homes don’t have A/C in summer)
  • Highlighter for marking your daily itinerary
Pro Tip — My #1 Europe Item A crossbody anti-theft bag. Between train stations, markets, and packed metro cars, it’s the single easiest way to travel with total peace of mind.
