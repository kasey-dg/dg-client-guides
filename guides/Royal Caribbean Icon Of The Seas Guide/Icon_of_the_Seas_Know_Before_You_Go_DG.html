Before You Sail
check-in & boarding

Royal Caribbean App

Download before you leave home

Most RC ships use the app to keep you connected to all cruise happenings, alongside the Cruise Compass. Download it early and connect your reservations — it's the best way to stay in the loop from the moment you board!

Online check-in opens 45 days before your sail date via the RC app or website. Website check-in closes 48 hours before sailing; app check-in stays open until 8am on the morning of sailing. The system times out after 30 minutes, so gather everything below before you start.

Check-In Checklist

Have these 6 things ready

  • ① Ship name, sailing date & Royal Caribbean reservation number
  • ② Passport or proof of citizenship & ID
  • ③ Home address
  • ④ Emergency contact name & phone number
  • ⑤ Travel plans before and after your cruise
  • ⑥ Credit card to register for your onboard expense account

Luggage Tags

Attach before you leave for the port

You'll receive luggage tags electronically in advance via the Royal Guest eDocs. Tags are ship-specific and include your sailing date, stateroom, and deck number. Attach them before you go — porters at the pier have blank backup tags if needed.

Porters handle your bags at drop-off — think curbside check-in at the airport! A tip of $1–$2 per bag is customary.

⚠ Don't pack these in checked luggage: Valuables, boarding documents, or anything you'll need in the first few hours. Luggage is delivered to staterooms in the afternoon/evening, so keep essentials in your carry-on!
🔗
Check-In Link

Check in online at royalcaribbean.com/account/signin. Once done, you'll choose your port arrival time — stick to it, as they may not allow early entry!

Day One
embarkation day

Finding the Ship

The RC app lists your ship's berth/pier. Large signs throughout the port also indicate which ship is docked where.

Parking at the Port

Most ports have on-site parking or nearby offsite options with a shuttle. Reserve ahead when possible — it's worth it!

Arrival Times

Earlier = more time to enjoy!

You'll select an arrival time during online check-in. Sticking to it keeps things running smoothly — arriving early may mean you can't enter yet. RC requires guests to be fully checked in and onboard no later than 90 minutes before port operations close.

Your Carry-On Bag

Pack this for embarkation day

  • Boarding pass screenshot & RC app
  • Passport / ID documents (do NOT pack in checked luggage!)
  • Medications
  • Valuables & electronics

When You Arrive

  1. Check luggage with a porter outside (tip them — $1–$2/bag!)
  2. Enter the cruise terminal and follow signage
  3. Go through security (similar to the airport)
  4. Check in with a guest agent — staff are everywhere to help
  5. Show your app and ID at several points along the way

Mandatory for All Guests
muster (safety) drill

What Is the Muster Drill?

The muster drill is a mandatory safety exercise at the start of every cruise. It shows guests where to go in an emergency, how to put on a life jacket, and other key safety measures.

Thanks to RC's eMuster™ technology, you can complete it at your own pace via the Royal Caribbean app or your stateroom TV. Before the ship departs, just visit your designated assembly station to have your SeaPass card scanned. No more set times or large group gatherings!

For Families with Children

Kids complete it a little differently

When an adult in the cabin watches the safety video and hears the emergency horn, children in that cabin are automatically considered complete for those steps. However, children must accompany adults to the assembly station to have their SeaPass cards scanned.

🐠
For Little Ones

Royal Caribbean has a kid-friendly safety video called "Learn about Safety with Lulu & Mika" — a fun, cartoon-style overview found at the bottom of the safety page on the app or stateroom TV.

The Final Day
disembarkation day

The Night Before

Late afternoon on your last full day, debarkation instructions and luggage tags will be delivered to your stateroom. Your account invoice will be delivered the morning of departure.

🏃 Self-Assist (Early Departure)

Carry your own bags off. Best if you have an early flight or want to be first off the ship.

  1. Keep luggage in your stateroom the night before — no tags needed
  2. Receive your invoice on departure morning — depart before the specified time
  3. Proceed to gangway with bags & SeaPass card, then through immigration & customs

Typically begins around 7:00–7:30am. Note: wheelchair assistance is not available for self-assist.

🧳 Regular Departure (Luggage Service)

Let the crew handle your bags. Great for large luggage or if you have plenty of time.

  1. Place tagged luggage outside your stateroom by 11:00pm on the last night
  2. Keep passport, SeaPass card, and valuables with you — never in the hallway bags
  3. Enjoy breakfast or relax in your departure lounge until your tag number is called
  4. Proceed to gangway, collect bags in the terminal by tag number, then clear customs
⚠ Don't forget: Keep your SeaPass card AND passport on your person when leaving the ship. You need both to exit and clear US Customs & Border Protection!
📺
Watch the Overview

A video walkthrough of the disembarkation process will be shown on your stateroom television the evening before departure. Worth a watch so you know exactly what to expect!

Home Port & Itineraries

Sailing year-round from Miami

Icon of the Seas sails 7-night Eastern and Western Caribbean itineraries departing from Miami. Every sailing includes a stop at Royal Caribbean's private island destination, Perfect Day at CocoCay in the Bahamas. Western Caribbean itineraries visit ports like Cozumel (Mexico), Roatán (Honduras), and others. Eastern Caribbean routes include stops like Philipsburg (St. Maarten).

Don't Be Intimidated by the Size

It's more manageable than you think

With 20 decks and 8 neighborhoods, Icon can sound overwhelming — but the ship is cleverly designed around distinct neighborhoods that naturally disperse guests and reduce crowding. Each neighborhood has its own dining, activities, and atmosphere, so you'll quickly find your favorite spots and settle in. Download the RC app before you board and review the deck plan so you can get oriented on embarkation day.

📱
Pro Tip — Explore on Embarkation Day

Spend time on embarkation day walking the ship before it gets busy. The neighborhoods each have a very different feel — you'll want to know where everything is before the crowds arrive. Pay special attention to where your dining reservations are located so you're not scrambling on night one!

Icon of the Seas
The 8 Neighborhoods

What Are Neighborhoods?

Instead of one giant ship, think of Icon of the Seas as eight distinct destinations — each with its own vibe, dining, activities, and atmosphere. This concept makes it easy to find exactly the experience you're in the mood for, whether that's high-octane thrills, peaceful relaxation, family fun, or fine dining.

🌊 Surfside

The ultimate family neighborhood

Designed as a "stay all day" destination for families with young kids. Features Splashaway Bay aqua park with slides and water cannons, a carousel, the Playscape dry playground, kid-friendly pools, a teens-only Social020 club, and multiple family dining options. Parents can relax at the Water's Edge infinity pool while the kids play nearby.

🏄 Thrill Island

The adrenaline hub

Home to Category 6 — the largest waterpark at sea — with 6 record-breaking water slides including Frightening Bolt (tall drop slide) and Pressure Drop (open freefall). Also features Crown's Edge (skywalk + zipline, extra fee), the FlowRider surf simulator, Adrenaline Peak rock climbing wall, Lost Dunes mini golf, and the Ultimate Abyss dry slide that drops 10 stories in near-total darkness.

🏝️ Chill Island

The relaxation retreat

Your go-to for poolside peace and tropical vibes. Features multiple pools including the Royal Bay Pool, the Cove Pool, and the adults-only Hideaway pool. El Loco Fresh (complimentary Mexican food) is located here — perfect for a quick bite between swims. If you just want to soak up the sun without the noise, Chill Island is your spot.

🌿 Central Park

The serene garden escape

A lush, open-air neighborhood in the heart of the ship filled with thousands of plants and live trees. Home to some of the best specialty dining on board — Chops Grille steakhouse, Izumi Hibachi & Sushi, Giovanni's Italian Kitchen, and Izumi in the Park (sushi to-go window). Lou's Jazz 'n Blues provides a live soundtrack. Park Café offers complimentary grab-and-go bites.

🎪 AquaDome

Entertainment & dining under a dome

A spectacular enclosed neighborhood featuring the AquaTheater, where world-class acrobatic shows take place including Aqua Action! (Cirque-style with robotic arms and a 55-foot water curtain) and the family-friendly Pirates vs. Mermaids. Also home to the AquaDome Market — the first food hall in the Royal Caribbean fleet with 5 globally-inspired dining stations.

🛳️ Royal Promenade

The lively main street

The ship's bustling main boulevard featuring shops, bars, entertainment, Sorrento's Pizza (complimentary), and the Schooner Bar. This is where you'll find parades, parties, and live entertainment throughout the voyage. Think of it as the ship's Times Square — always something going on.

👑 Suite Neighborhood

Exclusive luxury on four decks

Icon of the Seas has the largest suite neighborhood in the entire Royal Caribbean fleet, spanning four decks. Suite guests have access to Coastal Kitchen (Mediterranean-inspired private restaurant), The Grove (private sundeck with pool and whirlpool), and Concierge Club. If your clients are in suites, they have a ship within a ship!

🎭 Hideaway

Adults-only sanctuary

An exclusive adults-only retreat with its own pool, bar, and dedicated deck space. Perfect for guests who want to escape the family-friendly energy of the rest of the ship and enjoy a more peaceful, grown-up atmosphere. One of the most popular spots on sea days.

🗺️
Navigation Tip

Review the deck plan in the RC app before you board. The ship runs forward-to-aft with neighborhoods stacked across decks — once you get your bearings, it becomes very intuitive. The Royal Promenade on Deck 5 is a great central hub to orient yourself from.

Crown's Edge — Worth It?

Part skywalk, part ropes course, part zipline

Crown's Edge on Deck 16 is Icon's most talked-about thrill. You'll walk a skywalk obstacle course 154 feet above the ocean — and at some point, without warning, the floor drops and you zip back around the side of the ship. The experience lasts about 90 seconds and includes a GoPro video and photos. At roughly $89.99 per person, it's pricey for the duration, but for thrill-seekers it's unforgettable. Book in advance through Cruise Planner — it sells out.

AquaTheater Shows

Book early — these fill up fast

The AquaTheater inside the AquaDome is the most spectacular entertainment venue on the ship. Shows include Aqua Action! — a Cirque du Soleil-style performance featuring four robotic arms and a 55-foot water curtain — and the family-friendly Pirates vs. Mermaids. Arrive early to get a good seat as the AquaTheater fills up quickly. Check the RC app for show times as soon as you board.

Category 6 Waterpark

The largest waterpark at sea

Six incredible slides — from the thrilling Frightening Bolt tall drop slide, to the open freefall of Pressure Drop. No reservations required for the waterpark slides, but lines get long on sea days. Hit them first thing in the morning or during dinner hours for the shortest waits. All slides are complimentary and designed for both adults and kids (height requirements apply).

⏰
Best Times for Popular Activities

Waterslides, FlowRider, and rock climbing are busiest on sea days mid-morning to mid-afternoon. Head to these on embarkation day or during port days when most guests are off the ship — you'll walk right on with minimal wait!

📅

A Guaranteed Stop on Every Sailing
Perfect Day at CocoCay

Royal Caribbean's Private Island

Every Icon sailing stops here — plan ahead!

Perfect Day at CocoCay in the Bahamas is Royal Caribbean's award-winning private island destination and is included on every Icon of the Seas itinerary. It's not just a beach stop — it's a full day destination with its own waterpark, beach clubs, restaurants, and activities. Some experiences are included in your cruise fare, while others require advance purchase.

✓ Included at CocoCay

  • Beach access & loungers (first-come)
  • Swimming in the lagoon
  • Some complimentary food & non-alcoholic drinks
  • Kids splash areas
  • Basketball & volleyball courts
  • Walking & biking trails

✕ Extra Cost at CocoCay

  • Thrill Waterpark (13 slides, tallest in North America)
  • Coco Beach Club (overwater cabanas)
  • Hideaway Beach (adults-only)
  • Up, Up & Away helium balloon ride
  • Private cabana rentals
  • Zip line
  • Alcoholic beverages (unless drink package)
  • Snorkel & watersports rentals

Thrill Waterpark

The tallest waterslides in North America

CocoCay's Thrill Waterpark features 13 waterslides including Daredevil's Peak — the tallest waterslide in North America at 135 feet. A separate ticket is required (purchase in advance through Cruise Planner for the best price). If your clients love waterparks, this is an absolute must-do and worth every penny.

Coco Beach Club

Overwater luxury in the Bahamas

For a more elevated island experience, the Coco Beach Club offers the first overwater cabanas in the Bahamas, a stunning infinity pool, and a restaurant with sweeping ocean views. It's a premium add-on but makes for an incredibly special day. Book well in advance — this is one of the most sought-after experiences at CocoCay and sells out fast.

Up, Up & Away

A helium balloon adventure

Drift up to 450 feet above the island in a tethered helium balloon for breathtaking panoramic views of CocoCay and the surrounding Bahamas waters. It's a calmer, scenic experience — a great contrast to the waterpark if you're looking for something more relaxed but still memorable. Requires advance booking.

🏖️
CocoCay Pro Tips

Get off the ship early — beach chairs and the best spots go fast. If your clients have the Deluxe Beverage Package, it works at CocoCay too! And if the weather is rough, Royal Caribbean may substitute an alternate port — so don't pre-purchase too many add-ons until closer to sailing.

💰
Buy CocoCay Tickets in Advance

Thrill Waterpark, Coco Beach Club, and cabana rentals are all cheaper when booked ahead through Cruise Planner vs. purchasing on the island. Prices can be significantly higher on the day — sometimes 20–30% more.
